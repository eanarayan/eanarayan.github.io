Welcome to My Personal Website!

I am an experimental particle physicist currently working as a postdoctoral researcher at Southern Methodist University in Dallas. I work with the ATLAS experiment at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) in Switzerland, where we explore fundamental questions about the universe using data collected by our detectors.

My current research involves searching for a very rare process called di-Higgs production and developing advanced technology for the next-generation ATLAS detector, which will be used in future experiments at the LHC.

I earned my PhD in physics from the University of New Mexico in Spring 2024, under the guidance of Prof. Sally Seidel. My dissertation focused on measuring the lifetime of a particle called the B-meson, studying certain properties of its decays, and developing new sensors for particle detectors.
